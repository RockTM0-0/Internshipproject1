# Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) Project Report
**Project Title:** Advertising Spend vs. Sales Optimization  
**Generated Output:** Automatic Insights Analysis

## 1. Executive Summary
This project explores an advertising dataset consisting of budget allocations across three distinct media channels (**TV**, **Radio**, and **Newspaper**) and their resulting target variable (**Sales**). The goal of this analysis is to identify key trends, determine budget efficiencies, and isolate the major driving factors of company sales revenue.

---

## 2. Statistical Findings & Data Health
* **Total Observations:** 200 business periods/markets.
* **Missing Data:** Zero missing data entries were detected across all columns. The dataset is fully complete and pristine.
* **Data Scale:** Budget formats vary considerably. TV takes up the largest overall share of capital investments, whereas Newspaper displays highly skewed, lower-level budgeting distributions.

---

## 3. Key Influencing Factors & Trends (Core Insights)
Based on the computed matrix calculations and linear regressions, the marketing channels relate to sales performance as follows:

1. **🏆 Primary Driver (TV):** TV has a massive correlation score of **0.901** with overall sales performance. Incremental investments here show a steady, direct, and predictable upward surge in revenue generation.
2. **🥈 Secondary Driver (Radio):** Radio exhibits a moderate-to-strong positive baseline connection with sales ($r = 0.350$).
3. **⚠️ Underperforming Channel (Newspaper):** Spending on Newspaper features an incredibly weak correlation coefficient of **0.158**. The data shows a heavily scattered, non-linear trend, indicating that high newspaper budgets do not reliably result in higher sales.

---

## 4. Strategic Business Recommendations
* **Shift Capital Allocation:** Budget streams should prioritize **TV** and **Radio** campaigns where conversion trends remain tight and predictable.
* **Audit or Cut Newspaper Budgets:** Newspaper spending patterns display significant diminishing returns. Budgets here can likely be slashed or completely redirected to high-performing digital or video platforms without affecting baseline sales output.
